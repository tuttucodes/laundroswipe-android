Let's call the existing service "CityRide" and the new feature "Scheduled Rides." --- ## Project PRD/Brief: CityRide - Scheduled Rides Feature (V1.0 MVP) **Document Version:** 1.0 **Date:** October 26, 2023 **Author:** [Your Name/PM Team] **Status:** Draft / For Review --- ### 1. Executive Summary This document outlines the requirements for developing the "Scheduled Rides" feature for the CityRide mobile application (iOS & Android). The goal is to allow users to pre-book rides for a future date and time, providing greater convenience and reliability, especially for important appointments or travel. This MVP will focus on core scheduling functionality, basic management, and notifications. --- ### 2. Introduction & Problem Statement **2.1 Background:** CityRide is a leading on-demand ride-sharing platform. While our core strength is immediate ride availability, user feedback and market analysis indicate a significant demand for scheduled transportation options. Competitors offer similar features, and its absence is a frequently cited reason for users choosing alternative services for specific needs (e.g., airport transfers, doctor appointments). **2.2 Problem Statement:** Users currently lack the ability to book a ride in advance with CityRide. This forces them to rely on the uncertainty of on-demand availability for time-critical events, leads to anxiety, or pushes them to competitors who offer pre-booking services. This results in lost revenue opportunities for CityRide and a diminished user experience for a segment of our user base. --- ### 3. Goals & Objectives **3.1 Business Goals:** * **Increase User Retention:** Provide a more comprehensive service that meets a broader range of user needs. * **Acquire New Users:** Attract users who primarily need scheduled transport. * **Increase Average Ride Value:** Scheduled rides often have longer distances or fixed pricing, potentially increasing transaction value. * **Improve Driver Utilization:** Provide drivers with more predictable ride patterns during off-peak hours. * **Reduce Customer Support Inquiries:** Lessen calls related to "ride not available" during critical times. **3.2 Product Objectives (SMART):** * **By Q2 2024:** Launch the Scheduled Rides MVP for iOS and Android. * **Within 3 months of launch:** Achieve a 10% adoption rate among existing active users. * **Within 6 months of launch:** Capture 5% of all airport-related rides in key markets. * **Achieve >85%:** On-time pickup rate for scheduled rides within the first 6 months. * **Maintain <5%:** Cancellation rate for scheduled rides by drivers (post-assignment). --- ### 4. Target Audience * **Primary:** * **Business Travelers:** Need reliable transport to/from airports, meetings. * **Commuters:** Users with fixed schedules (e.g., daily work, school drop-offs). * **Event-Goers:** Attending concerts, sports events, requiring punctual arrival. * **Families/Caregivers:** Needing reliable transport for appointments (medical, school). * **Secondary:** Any existing CityRide user seeking peace of mind for future travel. --- ### 5. Scope (MVP - Minimum Viable Product) **5.1 In-Scope:** * **User Interface:** Integration into the existing CityRide app flow for selecting "Schedule a Ride." * **Ride Scheduling:** Ability to select a future date and time (up to 7 days in advance). * **Vehicle Type Selection:** Support for existing CityRide vehicle types (e.g., Standard, Premium, XL). * **Price Estimation:** Display a price estimate for the scheduled ride (may be dynamic closer to time or fixed). * **Booking Confirmation:** Clear confirmation screen with ride details. * **Ride Management:** * View upcoming scheduled rides. * Ability to cancel a scheduled ride (with policy defined). * **Notifications:** * Confirmation of scheduled ride. * Reminder before pickup (e.g., 30 mins, 15 mins). * Driver assignment notification. * Driver arrival notification. * **Driver Matching Logic:** Backend logic to match drivers to scheduled rides in advance, and re-match if necessary. * **Payment:** Use existing payment methods. Pre-authorization/holding of funds. **5.2 Out-of-Scope (for MVP, potential future phases):** * Recurring scheduled rides. * Multiple stop rides. * Ability to modify a scheduled ride (only cancel and re-book). * Specific driver requests. * Integration with calendar applications. * Advanced surge pricing for scheduled rides. * Dedicated "business account" scheduling. * In-app chat with assigned driver for scheduled rides. --- ### 6. Functional Requirements / Key Features * **FR1: Schedule Ride Entry Point:** User can access "Schedule a Ride" from the home screen/booking flow. * **FR2: Date & Time Picker:** Users can select a date (up to D+7) and time for pickup. * **FR3: Pickup & Destination Input:** Users input pickup and destination addresses. * **FR4: Vehicle Type Selection:** Users can choose from available CityRide vehicle types. * **FR5: Fare Estimation:** Display an estimated fare based on selected parameters. * **FR6: Booking Confirmation:** A dedicated screen to confirm all details before booking. * **FR7: Upcoming Rides View:** A section in the app (e.g., "My Rides" or "Scheduled") to view all future scheduled rides. * **FR8: Ride Cancellation:** Users can cancel a scheduled ride before a defined cut-off time (e.g., 1 hour prior). Cancellation fees to be defined. * **FR9: Driver Assignment:** Backend logic assigns a driver to the scheduled ride at an appropriate time prior to pickup. * **FR10: Push Notifications:** System sends relevant notifications (confirm, reminder, driver assigned, driver arrived). * **FR11: Payment Processing:** Process payment at the time of ride completion, pre-authorize at booking. --- ### 7. Non-Functional Requirements * **Performance:** * Ride scheduling confirmation within 3 seconds. * Notifications delivered within 60 seconds of trigger event. * **Security:** * All personal and payment data must be encrypted in transit and at rest. * Adhere to existing CityRide security protocols and industry best practices. * **Scalability:** * The system must be able to handle a 5x increase in scheduled ride volume without performance degradation within 2 years. * **Usability:** * Intuitive and seamless integration into the existing CityRide app UI/UX. * Adherence to iOS Human Interface Guidelines and Android Material Design principles. * **Reliability:** * High availability for scheduling service (99.9% uptime). * Robust error handling for driver matching and payment. --- ### 8. Success Metrics (KPIs) * **Scheduled Ride Completion Rate:** % of scheduled rides completed vs. booked. * **On-Time Pickup Rate:** % of scheduled rides where the driver arrives within +/- 5 minutes of the scheduled time. * **Feature Adoption Rate:** % of active users who have booked at least one scheduled ride per month. * **Cancellation Rate:** % of scheduled rides cancelled by user or driver. * **NPS Score:** Impact on overall user satisfaction (measure pre/post launch). * **Revenue from Scheduled Rides:** Total revenue generated by the feature. * **Customer Support Tickets:** Reduction in "ride availability" related issues. --- ### 9. Technical Considerations * **Backend Services:** Require new microservices for schedule management, driver assignment logic, notification triggers. * **Database:** Schema updates to store scheduled ride details. * **API Endpoints:** New APIs for scheduling, retrieval, cancellation, and driver matching. * **Mobile Development:** UI/UX changes for iOS and Android apps, leveraging existing components where possible. * **Notification Service:** Integration with existing push notification infrastructure. * **Payment Gateway:** Ensure compatibility with existing payment processing flows. * **Geospatial Services:** Leverage existing mapping and location services for precise pickups. --- ### 10. Design & User Experience (UX) Considerations * **Consistency:** Maintain CityRide's brand identity and existing design patterns. * **Clarity:** Clear communication of scheduling process, confirmation, and any associated policies (e.g., cancellation fees). * **Feedback:** Provide immediate visual feedback for user actions. * **Error States:** Clear and actionable error messages for failed bookings or cancellations. * **Accessibility:** Ensure the feature is accessible to users with disabilities. --- ### 11. Monetization Strategy * Initially, scheduled rides will follow existing pricing models (dynamic based on time of booking or fixed). * Potential for premium pricing for specific vehicle types or highly sought-after time slots in future iterations. * Cancellation fees (to be defined) for user cancellations within a specified window before pickup. --- ### 12. Future Phases / Roadmap (Post-MVP) * Recurring scheduled rides. * Ability to modify a scheduled ride (time, destination). * Integration with personal calendars (Google Calendar, Outlook). * "Favorite Schedule" option for frequent bookings. * Option for "wait and return" or multiple stops. * Enhanced surge pricing logic for scheduled rides during peak times. * Dedicated business accounts with admin scheduling. --- ### 13. Dependencies & Risks **13.1 Dependencies:** * Availability of dedicated mobile (iOS & Android), backend, and QA engineering teams. * Collaboration with data science for driver assignment algorithms. * Legal team review for terms of service, cancellation policies. * Marketing team for launch strategy and communication. **13.2 Risks:** * **Driver Adoption:** Drivers may initially resist accepting scheduled rides if they perceive it as less flexible or lucrative than on-demand. * *Mitigation:* Driver incentives, clear communication of benefits, pilot programs. * **Technical Complexity:** Integrating new scheduling logic with existing real-time systems without introducing latency or bugs. * *Mitigation:* Incremental development, robust testing, dedicated architectural review. * **User Expectations:** Users might expect 100% certainty for scheduled rides, which is hard to guarantee. * *Mitigation:* Clear communication on "estimated" pickup times, robust fallback options if a driver cancels. * **Competitive Response:** Competitors may enhance their scheduling features. * *Mitigation:* Continuous market monitoring, agile iteration. --- ### 14.
